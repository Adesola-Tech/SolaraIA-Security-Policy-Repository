# Password Construction Standard

## Policy Statement
SolaraIA requires strong password practices to protect systems, applications, and data from unauthorized access.

## Purpose
The purpose of this standard is to establish minimum password requirements that reduce the likelihood of account compromise.

## Scope
This standard applies to all users and accounts that authenticate to SolaraIA-managed systems, applications, and services.

## Password Requirements
Passwords must:
- Be at least 12 characters long
- Contain a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters where supported
- Not contain easily guessed words or personal information
- Not reuse previously used passwords where technically enforced

## Password Protection Requirements
Users must:
- Keep passwords confidential
- Never share passwords with other users
- Never store passwords in plain text
- Use approved password managers where permitted

## MFA Requirements
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be enabled for:
- Privileged accounts
- Remote access
- Administrative systems
- Critical business applications where supported

## Password Change Requirements
Passwords must be changed:
- When compromise is suspected
- When required by system configuration
- When issued temporary credentials are first assigned

## Prohibited Practices
The following are prohibited:
- Sharing passwords
- Writing passwords in visible or insecure locations
- Reusing company passwords on personal accounts
- Using default passwords beyond initial provisioning

## Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard may lead to access restrictions, disciplinary action, or further investigation.

## Review Cycle
This standard shall be reviewed annually and updated as authentication risks and best practices evolve.
